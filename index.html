    How We Test

    ← Back to Ratings

    Overview

    We combine controlled evaluations with real‑world trials. Each pair is reviewed on traction, stability, and usable design factors (fit, warmth, water handling). Scores are normalized to a 0–20 traction index, then mapped to simple tiers using the legend below.

    • Surface set: smooth ice, packed snow, mixed winter sidewalks.
    • Gait tasks: level walk, heel strike, turn, stop.
    • Design factors: outsole rubber/compound, lug depth & pattern, optional studs or spikes.

    Scoring

    1. Record traction & slip events across tasks.
    2. Weight by task difficulty; compute a 0–20 traction index.
    3. Assign tier: Low, Medium, High, Very High.

    Note: Spiked products may rate separately due to studded contact.

    Legend

    Traction Index → Tier

    • Very High (≥15) — ❄❄❄

      Confident traction on ice; strong stop/turn stability.

    • High (11–14) — ❄❄

      Reliable grip in most winter sidewalk conditions.

    • Medium (7–10) — ❄

      Adequate for packed snow; cautious on smooth ice.

    • Low (<7)

      Limited traction; avoid smooth ice without aids.

    Equipment

    • Ice/winter surface lane with temperature control
    • Harness for safety during slip trials
    • Task markers for walk / stop / turn cycles

    Protocol

    1. Warm‑up and sizing check
    2. Sequence: walk → heel strike → turn → stop
    3. Repeat runs; log slips and recoveries

    FAQ

    Do spiked products use the same tiers?

    We report spikes separately when studs dominate contact, but we still convert to the same 0–20 traction index for comparison.

    What else influences the score?

    Compound hardness, lug geometry, and temperature sensitivity all affect grip. Fit and insulation are noted but do not change the traction index.
