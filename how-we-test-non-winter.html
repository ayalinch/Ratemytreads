Non-Winter Testing Methodology

Our testing is the only kind in the world. Our scientists at The KITE Research Institute have developed a way to get reliable slip resistance results from having participants walk back and forth on a ramp while we slowly increase the angle of the floor. This process is called the Maximum Achievable Angle (MAA) test.

The Maximum Achievable Angle (MAA) Test – We Tip Till You Slip

Step 1: Lane Preparation

The test lane is prepared with the required surface (wet pavement or tile) to simulate real-world conditions.

Test Set

  • Surfaces: wet pavement, smooth tile, other slippery surfaces
  • Tasks: walk → heel-strike → turn → stop
  • Design checks: outsole compound, tread depth & pattern

Equipment

  • Temperature-controlled wet lane
  • Safety harness during slip trials
  • Markers for walk / turn / stop cycles

Protocol

  1. Fit check & warm-up
  2. Tasks: walk → heel-strike → turn → stop
  3. Repeat runs; log slips & recoveries
